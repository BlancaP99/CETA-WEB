COMPANY

COMPAÑÍA ESPAÑOLA DE TÉCNICA AERONÁUTICA S.A.

The company’s corporate purpose was focused on “...everything related to, or derived from, aeronautical engineering.” Headquartered on Doctor Esquerdo Street in Madrid, its operational base was located in Córdoba, at the San Jerónimo airfield (adjacent to the archaeological site of Medina Azahara).

Over 60 years of continuous evolution, CETA has achieved key milestones in General Aviation in Spain and progressively expanded its capabilities to provide technical support, operational availability, and fleet safety:

Key Milestones (1963–1990s)
  • Spain’s first major General Aviation maintenance centre: In February 1963, the Directorate General of Civil Aviation granted CETA Aeronautical Maintenance Centre Certificate No. 6, with capability to overhaul airframes, powerplants, propellers, accessories and instruments, supporting brands such as Piper, Cessna, Lycoming, Rolls-Royce, Hartzell, among others.
  • Spain’s first private avionics and instruments workshop: providing maintenance and support for avionics and instrumentation systems (King Radio, Narco, etc.).
  • CETA was one of the Spanish pioneering companies in aerial pest control and in aerial fire prevention and suppression operations.
  • Spain’s first private aerial photogrammetry operator: aircraft modified for aerial photogrammetry and mapping at multiple scales, including plotting/restitution systems for physical scale reconstruction of photogram positions.
Key Milestones (Air Taxi, Advanced Capabilities & RPAS)

As one of Spain’s earliest air-taxi operators, CETA introduced business jets (Learjet 25 and 35), operating a dedicated passenger-transport fleet and an operational service point in Barajas Terminal 2.

Another major milestone was the supply of aircraft to the Spanish Armed Forces, as well as the introduction of aviation GPS solutions, both panel-mounted and portable.

In the early 2000s, CETA consolidated a high-performance technical structure by bringing together leading industry professionals, enabling the delivery of high-complexity programmes.

CETA was the first Spanish company to introduce tactical RPAS (SUAS), deployed in 2007 to an operational theatre.

Present and Future

Today, CETA is an EASA-approved PART 21J Design Organisation (DOA 21J.635), focused on consultancy and engineering services delivering solutions in:

  • Aeronautical Engineering
  • Aircraft Modernisation Design and Certification
  • Avionics Systems
  • Mission Systems
  • Unmanned Aerial Systems
  • Unmanned Ground Systems
